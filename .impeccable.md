# Percelo — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Founders and operators running their own companies. They want signal fast, have low patience for complexity, and will bail if the interface feels like homework. They're checking in during a busy day — not studying a dashboard. The job to be done: understand quickly whether AI models mention their brand and what to do about it.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Confident, clear, credible.

Smart but not cold. The kind of tool a founder actually wants to open — not intimidating, not fluffy. It earns trust through clarity and precision, not through visual noise or enterprise weight. Think: a really well-designed audit result you'd actually want to share with your team.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light. Founders use this during the day, sharing screens, sending links to colleagues.
- **Tone**: Clean and deliberate — every element should feel chosen, not defaulted. Minimal decoration but never sterile.
- **References**: The current palette (off-white #FAFAF8, near-black #141414, warm tan borders #E5E2DC) is strong and should be preserved. The Geist type stack fits the technical-but-approachable register.
- **Anti-references**:
  - NOT academic/reporty — avoid walls of text, PDF-like layouts, whitepaper density
  - NOT generic SaaS — no purple gradients, glassmorphism, or glowing hero metrics
  - NOT cute/playful — no illustrations, emoji headings, excessive rounding

### Design Principles
1. **Signal over noise** — Show the most important thing first. Every secondary element should earn its place.
2. **Founders scan, not read** — Use strong visual hierarchy so the key takeaway is obvious in 3 seconds.
3. **Credibility through restraint** — The design should feel like the tool knows what it's doing. Confidence comes from clarity, not decoration.
4. **Shareable by default** — Reports will be shared with colleagues and clients. They should look good in a Slack screenshot.
5. **Data feels human** — Scores and numbers need context. Raw numbers without framing feel cold; framed numbers feel useful.
